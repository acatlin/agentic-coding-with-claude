Week 6 — Review: Owning the Diff

Week 5 taught the agent to write production code; this week teaches you to own what it wrote. The instruments: /diff's two views run after every task, four checkpoints that replace line-by-line reading, a six-mode taxonomy of how AI code characteristically fails, and /code-review's multi-agent findings read critically. Then the pair work — structured review comments a partner's agent can execute — and the principle underneath it all: understanding the changes is what it means to own your code, at a depth proportionate to how little you supervised the run.

Estimated total time: 3.5–4.5 hours (readings/videos + quiz + assignment + meeting). These pages cover the same ground as the assigned readings — use them as your primary lesson text, and the linked sources to go deeper.

This week's learning pages

Six mastery objectives, six lessons, in recommended order:

  • M31Apply
    Reading /diff: Full-Diff and Per-Turn Views

    The in-session diff panel: git's uncommitted-changes view vs. per-turn replay from the conversation, navigation, and the after-every-task-before-commit habit.

  • M32Apply
    The Four-Checkpoint Review Workflow

    Scope bounds before, approval gates during, the diff gate's four questions after, tests before merge — and why the gates together beat reading every line.

  • M33Analyze
    Six Failure Modes of AI-Generated Code

    Hallucinated imports, redundant logic, architectural drift, security gaps, style drift, happy-path-only handling — causes, diff tells, and a worked five-failure diff.

  • M34Apply
    Running /code-review and Interpreting Findings

    Scopes and targets, the multi-agent pipeline's verification step, severity tags, extended reasoning — and the protocol separating verified issues from noise.

  • M35Create
    Writing Actionable PR Review Comments

    The five-part comment (anchor, observation, consequence, request, severity), the verification bar, batching and nit caps — comments an agent can execute.

  • M36Evaluate
    Owning Your Code: Proportionate Review Depth

    Why understanding — not approval or authorship — is what ownership means, and sizing review depth to how little of the run you supervised.

Suggested path

Read in order — the week is one argument built in layers. M31 gives you the instrument and M32 the workflow around it; M33 loads the checklist that A6 names, so do its worked diff carefully. M34 and M35 are A6's two halves — machine findings in, structured comments out; draft M35's exercise before peeking. Finish with M36 before the quiz: it's the judgment lesson the other five instrument.

How this week is assessed

  • Quiz 6 (25 MCQs): the four checkpoints; the failure-mode taxonomy; interpreting /code-review findings; proportionate verification. Every lesson's self-check is written in the quiz's application-over-recall style.
  • Assignment A6 (15 pts, pairs): review your partner's Week 5 PR against the failure-mode checklist and leave structured, actionable comments. Your partner then directs Claude to address them and merges. Both roles are graded: quality of review given, and quality of response to review received.
  • Meeting 6: bring your A6 review — expect discussion of which failure modes you found, which findings you judged noise, and how you sized your review depth.
